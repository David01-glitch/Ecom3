5 Phones That Actually Make Sense for Older Parents

Most "smart" phones overwhelm older adults. We tested 14 models and found the five that strike the right balance between simplicity and capability.

Our top pick is the EasyDial Senior Phone — it skips touchscreens entirely in favor of large physical buttons that give a satisfying click. Each speed-dial slot holds a photo, so calling a grandchild is as easy as pressing their face.

For those who want something more capable, the GrandPad-style tablets we tested have one home screen, no app store, and a single "Help" button that connects to a real human in under 30 seconds.

Key takeaway: simpler isn't dumber — it's respectful design. The best phones we tested removed everything that wasn't essential and made the remaining features impossible to mess up.

Voice Assistants: Why They Beat Smartphones for Older Adults

No swiping, no menus, no tiny buttons. Just speak. Here's why a smart speaker is often the best first piece of "modern tech" for an older parent.

Voice assistants like the Echo Companion remove the biggest barriers older adults face with technology — fine motor coordination, small text, and confusing menus. "Call my daughter" or "play the radio" replaces a multi-step process with a single sentence.

Setup takes one of our advisors about 15 minutes over video. After that, the device essentially disappears into the home — sitting on a kitchen counter, ready whenever it's needed.

Best use cases: hands-free calls, daily news, weather, music, medication reminders, and emergency contacts. Skip: shopping features and skill stores — they add complexity without real benefit.
